5. Data deletion conditions

As part of our commitment to transparency and respect for your rights, we offer you the opportunity to understand and manage the retention of your data. The data deletion conditions we put in place aim to give you full control over the information you share with us. Please find below the main conditions we adhere to when deleting data, to ensure that your online experience meets your expectations:

  • User request :You may request the deletion of your data. This can be done via the contact form or by written request.
  • Shelf life : Data may be kept for a specific period of time necessary for the purpose for which it was collected. Once this period has expired, the data will be deleted, unless another legal basis justifies its retention.
  • Purpose of collection : Data will be deleted when the purpose for which it was collected is no longer applicable. If the data is no longer required to provide a service, respond to a request or achieve the original purpose, it will be deleted.
  • Revoked consent : If data has been collected on the basis of your consent and you revoke your consent, the associated data will be deleted, unless another legal basis justifies its retention.
  • Legal obligations :Data will be deleted if required by law, for example, in response to a legal request or in compliance with other legal obligations.

7. How are your data transferred ?

Now let's find out about the procedures and safeguards that govern the transfer of your data, to ensure that it is managed in compliance with current standards and regulations. Please note that privacy laws and standards, including the authorities' rights of access to your personal data, may differ from those of your country of residence:

  • Intra-EU transfers :Data transfers between EU countries are generally free, as they are all subject to the GDPR, guaranteeing a high level of data protection.
  • Transfers to countries with an adequacy decision : Some countries outside the EU have obtained an adequacy decision from the European Commission, meaning that they provide an adequate level of data protection. Data transfers to these countries are authorized without the need for additional measures.
  • Transfers to countries without an adequacy decision : In this case, we base ourselves on standard contractual clauses (SCC), binding company rules (BCR), or other mechanisms approved by data protection authorities.
  • Transfers to the United States :The Privacy Shield, which allowed US companies to self-certify to receive data from the EU, was invalidated in 2020. We will therefore rely on standard contractual clauses.
